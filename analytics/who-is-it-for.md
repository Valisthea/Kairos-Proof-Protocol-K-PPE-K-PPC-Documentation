# Who Is It For?

Kairos Analytics — and the underlying K-PPE infrastructure — serves any entity that needs verifiable on-chain data. This is **not** a single-product tool. It's infrastructure designed for an ecosystem of consumers.

## Primary Users

### DeFi Protocols & dApps
**Need**: Prove their metrics are accurate to users, investors, and regulators.
**Example**: A lending protocol integrates K-PPE to generate verifiable proofs of their collateral ratios. When a regulator asks "prove your TVL was $50M on March 1st," they can provide a Merkle proof with dual signatures — not a screenshot.

### DEXs & Trading Platforms
**Need**: Prove trade execution, volume accuracy, and price feed integrity.
**Example**: AsterDex or any perpetual DEX uses K-PPE to prove that a liquidation occurred at a specific price. Traders can independently verify the proof instead of trusting the platform's word.

### Institutional Investors & Funds
**Need**: Audit-grade records of on-chain positions and performance.
**Example**: A crypto hedge fund uses Kairos Analytics to generate provable track records. When presenting to LPs, they provide cryptographic proofs of every trade — not self-reported PnL.

### Auditors & Compliance Teams
**Need**: Independent verification of on-chain activity without trusting the audited entity.
**Example**: An audit firm uses the public verification endpoint to independently check proofs submitted by their client protocol. Zero trust required.

### DAOs & Governance
**Need**: Tamper-proof records of governance decisions.
**Example**: A DAO uses K-PPE to prove that a governance vote passed with 67% approval and 10,000 participating wallets. The proof is anchored on-chain and verifiable forever.

### Oracle Providers
**Need**: Prove the accuracy and timeliness of price feeds.
**Example**: An oracle provider uses K-PPE to generate proofs of every price update. If a protocol questions whether the oracle provided the correct price at a specific block, the proof is available.

### Analytics Competitors & Aggregators
**Need**: Verify data from other sources or provide verifiable data themselves.
**Example**: A data aggregator integrates K-PPE to differentiate itself by offering "verified analytics" — provable data that users don't need to trust blindly.

### Developers Building New Products
**Need**: Proof infrastructure without building it from scratch.
**Example**: A developer building a risk monitoring tool integrates K-PPE's SDK to generate proofs for every risk alert. Their alerts become verifiable facts, not just notifications.

## Geographic & Regulatory Context

Kairos Analytics is designed with European (MiCA) and international compliance in mind. The verifiable proof system is especially valuable in jurisdictions with strict data integrity requirements:

- **EU/MiCA**: Crypto asset service providers must maintain accurate, verifiable records
- **US/SEC**: Fund performance reporting requires auditable data trails
- **Global**: Any jurisdiction moving toward crypto regulation benefits from independently verifiable on-chain proofs
